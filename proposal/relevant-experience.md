# Relevant Experience

Our practice focuses on modernizing operational web applications for mid-market
distributors and manufacturers, teams for whom the software is not the product, but
running the business day to day depends on it.

**Representative engagement profile.** Our typical project for this segment involves
inheriting a partially-built internal tool from a prior vendor or in-house effort:
inconsistent frontend patterns, missing test coverage, and a backlog of
operations-reported defects. We stabilize the existing system first (defect
remediation, test coverage), then layer in the specific operational capability the
business actually asked for, in Meridian's case a restocking recommendation engine
built from stock, demand, and budget data already sitting in the system.

**Why that maps to this engagement.** The pattern in this RFP is a familiar one to
us: a functional but incomplete 2024 build, a testing gap blocking IT sign-off, and
one specific feature (Restocking) that the operations team has been waiting on. We
are not proposing an unfamiliar approach here, we are proposing the sequence we have
found actually works: stabilize, then build, with test coverage carried throughout
rather than treated as a separate deliverable at the end.

**References available on request**, including engagements involving multi-region
operations teams (comparable to Meridian's SF, London, and Tokyo footprint) and
i18n/localization work for non-English-primary users.
